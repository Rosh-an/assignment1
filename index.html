Our Mission

To work with other communities to minimize hunger, poverty and maximize care for both the living creatures residing on earth and the environment which provides us a place to live in. We also monitor the rural areas and slums in India to improve the quality of health, cleanliness and living in such areas. Apart from these, women empowerment is a key objective for our team.

Our work

Plantation

Serve plants saplings and supports them to become trees. At Serve we aim to increase the area covered by plantation and trees, help famers with fruit plantations, clean and rejuvenate lakes in rural areas and make villages self sufficient using solar power technology. However, serve does it with the help of many inviduals both local and remote, indian and multinational organizations.

Education

Education is the reason behind growth of devloping countries and Serve tries its best to help the country with educating children belonging to poor families. Serve takes the initiative to make arrangements of providing free education to girl children. All these help in increasing knowledge about the world, increase awareness among children and teens, help achieve poor people achieve their dreams and increase the quality of living in rural areas. Serve works with many individuals and organizations to achieve the same. Serve is always committed towards the empowerment of rural children.

Articles

  • A Social Worker's Thoughts on Child Protection Social Work, by Cathy Lipke, from our Winter 2016 issue. "Each day is different, and each case calls for different skills." Takeaway: child protection work is the best job ever.



  • Mindfulness: 10 Lessons in Self-Care for Social Workers, by Deborah Lisansky Beck, from our Winter 2016 issue. "Mindfulness is not a test of your skills, nor is it a competition. There are no final exams or papers to write. Relate to yourself with a sense of caring, curiosity, open-mindedness, and acceptance." Takeaway: One size does not fit all when it comes to self-care



  • The Impostor Syndrome and the Social Worker, by Danna Bodenheimer, from Real World Clinical Blog. "Whereas feelings of being a fraud or impostor are commonly associated with being a new social worker, these psychological states are actually endemic to clinical social work practice across all stages of experience. Some social workers talk openly about feeling like an impostor, while others keep their feelings private and to themselves." Takeaway: If we recognize external factors that "conspire" to make us feel "less than," a truer sense of self can evolve.
